# Office Add-in design language

The Office design language is a clean, simple visual system that keeps the experience consistent across Office applications. It defines the visual elements used in Office interfaces:

- A standard typeface
- A common color palette
- A set of typographic sizes and weights
- Icon guidelines
- Shared icon assets
- Animation definitions
- Common components

[Fluent UI](add-in-design.md) is the official front-end framework for building with the Office design language. Using Fluent UI is optional, but it's the fastest way to make your add-in feel like a natural extension of Office.

## Balance your brand with Office

Many Office Add-ins are associated with a preexisting brand. You can keep your brand identity — including its visual and component language — while integrating with Office. Look for opportunities to swap out Office colors, typography, icons, or other stylistic elements with your own brand elements, while still following common add-in layouts and UX design patterns.

A heavily branded HTML UI inside Office can create dissonance for users. Find a balance that fits seamlessly in Office but clearly aligns with your service or parent brand. When an add-in feels out of place, it's usually because stylistic elements conflict. For example, typography that's too large and off grid, colors that are too contrasting or loud, or animations that behave differently than Office. Controls and components that veer too far from Office standards also break the visual consistency.
